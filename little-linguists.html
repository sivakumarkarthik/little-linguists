👨‍👩‍👧 For grown-ups

Welcome! This little app helps children learn first words in French, English, Mandarin, Tamil and Arabic. Tap any sticker and it speaks the word aloud. A few tips to get the best out of it:

🔊

Wake up the sound. Tablets and phones keep audio asleep until the first tap. Tap Léo the snail (or his "Tap me!" bubble) once and everything will talk after that.

🐢

Turtle button = slow speech. Great for hearing each sound clearly. The rabbit returns to normal speed.

🔁

×2 Repeat mode is the practice trick. Each word plays once at normal speed, pauses, then plays again slowly. The pause is your child's turn — encourage them to say the word out loud before the slow repeat, then copy it again after. Hearing it, saying it, and hearing it again is how little ones really learn.

📱

Install it like an app — tablets & phones. Open the link in your browser, then: iPad/iPhone (Safari): tap the Share button (square with arrow) → Add to Home Screen → Add. Android (Chrome): tap menu ⋮ → Add to Home screen (or Install app) → Add. Your child gets a tappable icon that opens full screen with no browser bars.

💻

Install it like an app — computers. Chrome: menu ⋮ → Cast, save and share → Install page as app (or Create shortcut, ticking "Open as window"). Edge: menu ⋯ → Apps → Install this site as an app. It then appears in the Start menu or dock like any other app.

🗣️

Voices. French and English voices are built into nearly every device. Mandarin, Tamil and Arabic depend on what's installed — if a word sounds wrong or silent, add the voice in your device's settings (usually under Accessibility → Spoken Content or Text-to-speech), which takes about a minute.

🛜

Works offline. Once the page is open, no internet is needed. Stars reset each visit — they're just for fun!
